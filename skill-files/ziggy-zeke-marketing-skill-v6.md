## THE CORE TRANSFORMATION — THE HEART OF ALL MARKETING

This is the emotional engine behind every piece of content you write. Always return to this:

**Before reading Ziggy & Zeke:**
A child gets mad at a friend → holds onto it → the anger festers → grows into resentment, anxiety, and prolonged unhappiness. Nobody taught them it's OK to feel mad AND let it go.

**After reading Ziggy & Zeke:**
A child gets mad at a friend → recognizes the feeling → learns it's better to say "I'm sorry" → deals with the emotion at the moment → moves forward happier and emotionally stronger.

**The key insight Jeff lived personally:**
"When I was a child who was neglected — when I got mad, I stayed mad. Nobody hugged me and said it's OK to be mad, but you have to let it go and deal with the emotion at the moment. I wrote this book because I didn't have it. Your child can."

**This is Jeff's most powerful marketing truth:**
He didn't just write a book about forgiveness — he lived the opposite of it as a neglected child, and that personal pain is what makes this book real, urgent, and different from every other children's book about feelings.

**The core message in one sentence:**
"I wrote this book because nobody hugged me and said it's OK to be mad — but you have to let it go. Ziggy & Zeke teaches children what I had to learn the hard way."

**The children's book market dynamic:**
The buyers (parents, grandparents, teachers, librarians) are NOT the readers (kids). Marketing always targets adults actively searching for high-quality, values-driven books for the children in their lives.

## THE THREE AUDIENCES — Write Differently for Each

### AUDIENCE 1: PARENTS
**Who they are:** Parents of children ages 5–10, concerned about emotional outbursts, sibling fights, friendship conflicts, bullying, social anxiety. They want practical tools, not just cute stories.
**What keeps them up at night:** "My child doesn't know how to handle being mad. They either explode or shut down. I don't know how to teach them."
**What they want to feel:** Hopeful, understood, equipped
**Key emotional hook:** Jeff's personal story of growing up without emotional guidance
**Call to action:** Buy on Amazon → read together tonight
**Tone:** Warm, personal, story-driven, parent-to-parent
**Sub-audiences to target:** Homeschool moms, gentle parenting advocates, parents of strong-willed children

### AUDIENCE 2: TEACHERS & EDUCATORS
**Who they are:** Elementary school teachers (K-5), school counselors, social-emotional learning (SEL) coordinators, librarians, after-school program directors, homeschool co-op leaders
**What they care about:** Classroom conflict resolution, SEL curriculum standards (CASEL-aligned), tools that give them real examples to discuss with students, books that spark meaningful conversation
**What they want to feel:** That this book does real pedagogical work, not just entertain
**Key hook:** "Real-life story examples that teach emotional regulation — CASEL-aligned and ready to use in any classroom discussion"
**Call to action:** Order for classroom, recommend to school library, include in SEL curriculum, invite author visit
**Tone:** Professional, evidence-aware, results-focused, collegial
**Resource advantage:** Full educator toolkit available FREE at ziggyandzeke.com/educators (Lesson Plan, Activity Pack, Discussion Cards, Read-Aloud Guide)

### AUDIENCE 3: CHILDREN WHO STRUGGLE (via parents/educators)
**Who they are:** Children ages 5–10 who bottle up anger, act out, have trouble with friendships, or struggle to apologize
**What they need:** A character they can see themselves in — someone who feels what they feel and shows them the way through
**Key hook:** Ziggy and Zeke show — don't just tell — how to move from mad to forgiven
**Tone when writing to this audience (via parents/educators):** Compassionate, validating, hopeful

### AUDIENCE 4: MICRO-INFLUENCERS (NEW — v6)
**Who they are:** Homeschool moms (3K–10K followers), child therapists, gentle parenting coaches, pediatric occupational therapists, school counselor content creators
**Why they matter:** High engagement rates, trusted voices with exact target audience, more authentic than paid celebrities
**Outreach approach:** DM-based, offer free book copy in exchange for honest review/post
**Best platforms to find them:** Instagram, TikTok
**Message tone:** Genuine, peer-to-peer, no hard sell — lead with the book's mission
**Sample DM opener:** "Hi [Name] — I'm Jeff, a first-time author and 61-year-old broadcast veteran who wrote a children's book about friendship and forgiveness because I was the angry kid who needed it. I think your audience would connect with it. Would you be open to a complimentary copy?"

### Systematic School Outreach Process
**Step 1 — Build Target List:** Spreadsheet of all elementary schools and public libraries within 50-mile radius. Include school name, type, librarian name/email, principal name/email. Start with 50 targets.

**Step 2 — Initial Email:** Send personalized email to school librarian (primary gatekeeper). Attach Lesson Plan and Activity Pack. Offer FREE review copy + complimentary virtual author visit. Lead with value, NOT sales.

**Step 3 — Follow Up:** One week after initial email. Brief follow-up. Relationship building, not closing. Most sales happen after follow-up.

**Step 4 — Conduct the Visit:** 20-30 minute virtual or in-person session. Read from book, share frog rescue video origin story, Q&A with students. Send pre-order flyers home before the visit.

**Step 5 — Secure Bulk Order:** After successful visit, discuss school library copies, classroom sets, or school bookstore. Present bulk pricing tiers.

**Step 6 — Reading Lists:** Even without immediate purchase, ask to be added to recommended reading list or summer reading program.

### Library-Specific Strategy (URGENT — Spring Window)
**⚠️ CRITICAL TIMING: Libraries plan summer reading programs in MARCH and APRIL — outreach must happen NOW**

- Contact children's department directly
- Offer to lead a storytime session
- Libraries are MORE receptive to self-published authors than schools
- Bring copies to sell and sign at events
- Ensure book is available through **IngramSpark** and **Baker & Taylor** (primary library distributors)

### School Outreach Timing
- **Schools:** September through November (best window)
- **Libraries:** March and April (summer reading planning — URGENT)
